Four self-contained demos for a one-day session: image recognition in the morning, language models and retrieval in the afternoon. Everything runs in your browser, no login, no API keys, no data leaves the tab. Open them in order, or jump straight to the one you need.

Image Classifier

Show a real convolutional network (ResNet-50) a photo, a balloon, an airliner, a geyser, an aurora, Earth from space, and watch it turn the pixels into a ranked spread of probabilities over its 1,000 everyday labels. It nails the ordinary objects but has no word for the atmospheric-science scenes it never trained on. Blur, rotate, greyscale or pixelate the image, or drop in your own, and see the guess move.

Build a Filter by Hand

Open up the classifier: a CNN is built from one operation, sliding a small grid of numbers (a filter) over the image. Edit the nine numbers and the actual JavaScript convolution loop, and watch the feature map light up on edges, textures and blurs. The equivalent NumPy Python is shown alongside. A hands-on coding exercise, no model needed.
